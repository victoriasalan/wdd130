History

White water rafting has come a long way from its humble and rugged beginnings. Its roots trace back to the early 19th century, when wooden rafts were used for exploration and military expeditions. In 1842, Lieutenant John Fremont led one of the first documented rafting journeys down the Platte River, aided by famed guide Kit Carson.

The sport as we know it began to take shape in the mid-20th century with the development of rubber inflatable rafts, initially created for military use in World War II. By the 1960s and '70s, outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers had transformed rafting into a recreational activity, launching commercial trips down rivers like the Colorado, Snake, and Gauley.
